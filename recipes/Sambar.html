Sambar

Description

Sambar is a traditional South Indian lentil-based vegetable stew. It is a flavorful and nutritious dish made with a variety of vegetables and aromatic spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (drumsticks, carrots, beans, pumpkin, etc.), chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup tamarind extract
  • 1 teaspoon sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 dry red chilies
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Wash and cook toor dal in a pressure cooker until soft. Mash and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, combine chopped vegetables, tamarind extract, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until vegetables are tender.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dry red chilies, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Saute until mustard seeds pop.
  4. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and mushy.
  6. Transfer the tempering mixture to the pot with cooked vegetables.
  7. Add mashed toor dal to the pot and mix well. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Sambar is ready to be served with steamed rice or idli/dosa!
